Disutils Work Experience

Around when I started my degree, me and some of my friends decided on making a service for the popular social media app - Discord, which specialized in making bots for various purposes to enhance user experience on the app. We made bots related to music, moderation, games, etc.

I was the lead developer for all our bots. I had brought about a lot of code quality improvements and optimizations. There were many challenges and road blocks that we had hit but we managed to come up with unique solutions to get by them. One of the more challenging problems we had faced was, repeating code in all our bots. All of our bots had the same core features, like a help command, embeds system, cooldown system, a common base view, etc. The problem was that if we ever had to make any changes to these common features in any one of these bots, we'd have to copy paste those changes in all of our bots. This was a tedious process and was error prone.

That's when I had come up with a solution of taking all the core common systems - modularizing them and package them into an open source library, called disckit. We then simply make that library into a dependancy for all our bots. This solved all of our issues with repeating code & maintainability. Other than just solving our problem, the library also became quite popular, stacking up +16k installs.

After making and updating our bots for more than a year, we finally decided to shutdown our service on December of 2025. Mainly because most of our team members got busy in their own life and academics, including me. But in the end, all of us came out of this project as much better developers.
